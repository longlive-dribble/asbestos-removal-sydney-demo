Asbestos removal in North Shore

The North Shore is largely brick and leafy, with solid older homes through Chatswood, Gordon and up towards Hornsby. Asbestos here tends to hide in the details rather than the whole house: eaves and soffits, garage and shed walls, bathroom and laundry sheeting, and the fibro additions and studios tucked into big backyards. Pockets around Ryde and the older Hornsby streets still have plenty of fibro homes.

Disposal and your local council

The North Shore covers several councils, including Willoughby, Ku-ring-gai, City of Ryde and Hornsby Shire. Each has its own waste rules, but none of them accept asbestos in normal collections. It must be wrapped, taken to a licensed facility and disposed of with a record kept, which your removalist arranges.

A homeowner can remove up to 10 square metres of bonded asbestos unlicensed, but a full garage lining or a Gordon home's run of eaves can reach that quickly. Anything friable, or any larger job, legally needs a licensed removalist.

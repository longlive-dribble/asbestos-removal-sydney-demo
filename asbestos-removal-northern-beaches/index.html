Asbestos removal in Northern Beaches

The Northern Beaches is full of the classic Sydney fibro beach shack. From the older cottages around Manly and Brookvale to the weekenders that dot Mona Vale and the streets behind Dee Why, a lot of homes built before the 1980s used bonded asbestos sheeting in their walls, eaves and ceilings. Older garages, carports and backyard sheds are the usual suspects, often the first thing a renovation uncovers.

Disposal and your local council

Removal and disposal here falls under Northern Beaches Council. Asbestos waste cannot go in a normal bin or skip. It has to be wrapped, labelled and taken to a facility licensed to accept it, with the paperwork to prove where it ended up. Your removalist sorts the tip run and the disposal record so you are not left holding the risk.

In NSW you can remove up to 10 square metres of bonded asbestos from your own home without a licence. A single fibro shed wall or a run of eaves on a Dee Why cottage can pass that in one go, and anything friable needs a licensed removalist regardless of size.

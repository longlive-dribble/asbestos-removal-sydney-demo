The rules in NSW

Know the law before you touch it

A quick, plain-English guide so you know where you stand before any work starts.

The 10 square metre rule

In NSW a homeowner may remove up to 10 square metres of non-friable (bonded) asbestos from their own home without a licence. Go above 10 square metres, or touch any friable asbestos, and a licensed removalist is legally required.

Even under 10 square metres, most people hand it to a licensed removalist for safe handling and disposal. All asbestos work in NSW is regulated by SafeWork NSW.

The two licence classes

A Class B licence covers bonded (non-friable) asbestos: the sheeting in eaves, fences, sheds and walls. A Class A licence covers friable asbestos as well as bonded, the crumbly, higher-risk material.

Ask to see the licence before any work starts. A licensed removalist will always be happy to show it.

General guidance based on SafeWork NSW rules. Always confirm the current requirements at safework.nsw.gov.au.

Common questions

Asbestos removal in Sydney, answered

Can I remove asbestos myself in NSW?
In NSW a homeowner may remove up to 10 square metres of non-friable (bonded) asbestos from their own home without a licence. Above 10 square metres, or for any friable asbestos, a licensed removalist is legally required. Even for small jobs, many people choose a licensed removalist for safe handling and disposal.
What is the difference between a Class A and Class B asbestos licence?
A Class B licence covers bonded (non-friable) asbestos, such as sheeting in eaves, fences, sheds and walls. A Class A licence covers friable asbestos as well as bonded, meaning the crumbly, higher-risk material. Both are issued under SafeWork NSW.
How is asbestos disposed of in Sydney?
Asbestos waste cannot go in your general bin. It must be double-wrapped in heavy plastic, labelled, and taken to a waste facility that is licensed to accept asbestos. A licensed removalist handles the wrapping, transport, tip fees and disposal paperwork, and knows which facilities in your area take it.
